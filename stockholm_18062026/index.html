Salon AI · Stockholm · 18 June 2026

Can Europe have it all?

Europe may become the first economy governed as a circular system. The question is whether this becomes its competitive advantage — or its constraint.

A private evening for people working at the edge of that question, with AI as the analytical medium.

Private salon · Curated group · By invitation

The evening's question

Growth, resilience, competitiveness.
Can Europe achieve all three through circularity — or only at the expense of one another?

Europe lacks the scale advantages of the United States, the energy economics of China, the frontier-tech velocity of either. What it may dominate is systems regulation — and industrial circularity. Whether that is a competitive position, or a constraint by another name, is the unresolved question of the next decade.

The premise

Regulation, interpreted as a system.

The European Union has written the most ambitious circular economy framework in the world. Whether it translates into economic strength remains unanswered — including by the institutions that wrote it.

The evening reframes that question. What happens when regulation is not only implemented, but read as a system — by AI capable of interpreting it at the scale of the economy that has to live inside it?

AI as interpretive infrastructure, not automation tool. That is the analytical premise of the evening.

How the evening operates

Two movements, one question.

The first part of the evening is hands-on. Each guest builds an analytical AI assistant fitted to their own work — not as an AI lesson, but as the operating instrument for the rest of the evening.

In the second part, we turn the assistants outward — onto Europe's circular framework. Where regulation meets economic logic. Where ambition meets constraint. The assistants are used to interrogate, not to illustrate.

You leave with a sharper model of Europe's transition economy — and AI systems capable of interrogating it alongside you.
