Salon AI · Stockholm · 18 June 2026

Europe, competitiveness, and the circular economy in the age of artificial intelligence.

There's a version of this evening where you leave with a tote bag, a hangover, and an unshakeable feeling that someone, somewhere, should really be doing something about Europe. We're doing the version where you leave with a working agent, a good understanding about Europe and the circular economy and what your role in it…

…ready for Midsummer ;)

The evening's question

Can Europe Have It All?
Growth, Resilience, and Competitiveness in a Circular, AI-Enabled Economy?

Let's discuss it. With AI.

How the evening works

65% The first part is selfish. You build your own AI agent, for your own work. No coding. Experts the whole way. You leave knowing what AI can actually do, because you made it do something.

35% Then we point the agents at Europe. Competitiveness and the circular economy. The EU has written the most ambitious circular framework in the world. Whether any of it turns into money is unclear. We spend an evening finding out what AI thinks.

The context

Regulation, interpreted as a system.

The European Union is advancing one of the most ambitious circular economy frameworks globally. At the same time, a fundamental question remains unresolved: how regulatory ambition translates into economic strength.

This evening shifts the question into an operational space: what happens when regulation is not only implemented, but interpreted as a system by AI?
